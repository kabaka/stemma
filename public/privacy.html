Stemma

Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 18, 2026

Stemma has no backend and no accounts. It does not collect, transmit, sell, or share your personal or health information with us or anyone else. There is no Stemma server that could receive your data, and there is no advertising or analytics.

Where your data lives

Everything you enter or import is stored locally in your own browser (in localStorage) on your device. You control it: you can view, edit, export it to open standards, or delete it at any time. Clearing your browser's site data for Stemma removes it.

Network connections Stemma makes

Stemma runs in your browser and only ever makes two kinds of outbound request, both under your control:

  • Optional terminology lookup. When you search for a medical condition, the search term you type may be sent to the U.S. National Library of Medicine's public clinical-terminology service to fetch matching codes. No personal or health data is sent — only the term you type.
  • Optional health-record import (SMART on FHIR). If you choose to connect to a healthcare provider, your data flows only between your browser and the provider's own FHIR server that you selected. It does not pass through any Stemma server or third party. You authenticate directly with your provider, and access is read-only.

Access tokens

When you connect to a provider, the temporary access token is held in your browser's session storage and is discarded when you close the tab. A longer-lived refresh token is stored only if you explicitly choose "stay connected on this device," and disconnecting wipes all stored tokens and the connection immediately. Tokens are never sent anywhere except back to your chosen provider.

Storage is not encrypted at rest

Because your data is stored in your browser profile, it is kept in plain text on your device and could be read by someone with access to that device or profile (or by a malicious browser extension). Treat the device you use Stemma on as trusted, and use your operating system's account and disk protections.

Children

Stemma is a general-purpose tool intended for use by adults managing their own and their family's health information. It does not knowingly collect information from anyone, since it collects nothing at all.

Changes
